## Slide 2 — Research Question (~45 sec)

My research question is:

**How does an AI-assisted interactive website affect young women's ability to identify and reframe gendered narrative bias in traditional BaZi and Western astrological readings?**

**After using Reframe Destiny, young women will show increased awareness of gender bias in divination narratives, and greater confidence in reframing these narratives for themselves.**

## Slide 3 — Background (~1 min)

Many young women encounter BaZi or astrology through family, social media, or apps.

These systems do not just describe personality. They tell stories about marriage, morality, and life paths.

As a female student who studies BaZi, I noticed something troubling: the **same chart element** can mean "ambitious leader" for men, but "too strong" or "harmful to husband" for women.

This project does **not** ask whether divination is true. It asks: **Who gets to narrate your fate — and does that narration carry gender bias?**

Reframe Destiny is my answer. It is a critical interactive tool — **not** a fortune-telling app.

//

---

## Slide 4 — Literature Review (~1 min 15 sec)

I build on four key sources.

**Butler (1990)** argues gender is performed through cultural narratives. Divination labels like "good wife" are gender performance.

**Foucault (1972)** shows discourse shapes what counts as knowledge. Fortune-telling language can discipline women without sounding political.

**Haraway (1988)** reminds us: who interprets data matters. The same birth chart means different things in different hands.

**Bender et al. (2021)** warn that AI reproduces bias. In my project, AI is a **lens for critique** — not an oracle.

The gap: no existing tool lets users **compare gender perspectives** on the same chart and **actively reframe** the narrative. That is my contribution.

//

---

## Slide 5 — Research Design / Method (~1 min 15 sec)

My research artifact is Reframe Destiny — a bilingual website with a seven-step Journey.

Users choose BaZi or astrology, enter birth data, read a traditional interpretation, run a **Bias Scanner** across seven categories, join a **Court of Destiny** dialogue, and see an **AI Reframe** side by side with the original.

I plan to test **15 to 25 young women**, aged 17 to 34, using a pre-survey, a 15-minute session, a post-survey, and five short interviews.

I will also code 40 traditional readings for seven bias types: gender bias, marriage centrism, fatalism, fear narrative, moral judgment, heteronormativity, and class narrative.

**Important:** I have **not yet run** the user study. Midterm shows the instrument and expected outcomes only.
