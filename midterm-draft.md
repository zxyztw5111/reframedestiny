## Research Question & Hypothesis

**Research Question:** How does an AI-assisted interactive website affect young women's ability to identify and reframe gendered narrative bias in traditional BaZi and Western astrological readings?

**Hypothesis:** I hypothesize that after using Reframe Destiny, young women will show increased awareness of gender bias in divination narratives and greater confidence in reframing these narratives for themselves.

---

## Background

Many young women in China and globally encounter BaZi or astrology through family, social media, or apps. These systems do not just describe personality — they tell stories about marriage, morality, and life paths.

As a female student who studies BaZi, I noticed the same chart element can mean "ambitious leader" for men but "too strong" or "harmful to husband" for women. This is not a small wording difference. It can shape self-confidence and how women relate to traditional culture.

This project does not ask whether BaZi or astrology is "true." It asks: **who gets to narrate your fate, and does that narration carry gender bias?**

Reframe Destiny is my answer — an interactive website, not a fortune-telling app.

---

## Literature Review

**Gender as narrative (Butler, 1990).** Butler argues gender is performed through repeated cultural stories, not a fixed inner trait. Divination readings are one such storytelling system — they perform gender through labels like "good wife" or "ke-fu."

**Discourse and power (Foucault, 1972).** Foucault shows that discourse controls what counts as acceptable knowledge. Traditional fortune-telling language is discourse: it can discipline women into acceptable roles without claiming to be political.

**Situated knowledge (Haraway, 1988).** Haraway argues that who interprets data matters. The same birth chart read by a traditional text, an AI, and the user herself produces different meanings — my project makes this visible.

**AI ethics (Bender et al., 2021).** Large language models can reproduce cultural bias. In my project, AI is a **lens for critique**, not an oracle — it helps reframe language, not validate fate.

**Gap:** Existing tools deliver traditional readings unchanged. No interactive product lets users switch gender perspective on the same chart and then co-create a reframed narrative. That is my contribution.

## Research Design / Method

**Artifact:** Reframe Destiny — a bilingual (Chinese/English) interactive website with a 7-step "Journey": choose system (BaZi or astrology) → choose lens → enter birth data → traditional reading → Bias Scanner (7 categories) → Court of Destiny (scholar / AI / user dialogue) → AI Reframe with bias fragment collection.

**Participants (planned):** 15–25 young women, aged 17–34, recruited from school peers and online communities.

**Procedure:** Pre-survey (5 Likert items on bias awareness) → 15-min website session → post-survey → optional 5-min interview for 5 participants.

**Measures:** Change in pre/post survey scores; which bias categories users identify; qualitative themes from Court of Destiny reflections.

**Content analysis (ongoing):** Coding 20 traditional BaZi readings and 20 astrology readings for 7 bias types: gender bias, marriage centrism, fatalism, fear narrative, moral judgment, heteronormativity, class narrative.

**Note:** User study has **not yet been run**. Midterm presents the instrument and expected outcomes only.

## Preliminary Results — Expected Results — user study not yet run

I have **not yet collected user data**. Based on my prototype testing and literature review, I **expect**:

1. Users will identify **marriage centrism** and **gender role stereotypes** most frequently in traditional readings
2. Pre/post survey scores on "I can recognize gender bias in divination language" will **increase** after one session
3. The side-by-side traditional vs. reframed reading will feel more **empowering** than the traditional reading alone
4. Users may still find the interface complex on first visit — the mentor's "mini-game level" format may lower this barrier

**Prototype evidence (informal, n=1–2):** Self-testing confirms the 7-step flow works; Bias Scanner radar chart displays 7 categories; 3 bias fragments unlock per journey session.
