# Research Summary

## What is already known

Traditional divination systems (BaZi in Chinese culture, Western astrology globally) function as **narrative frameworks** — they do not merely "predict" but **tell stories** about who a person is and what their life should look like. Feminist and discourse theorists (Butler, Foucault, Haraway) argue that such narratives can **reproduce gender norms** rather than describe neutral truths.

In BaZi specifically, the same chart elements receive **different interpretations by gender**. For example, strong "比肩劫财" (Peer/Robbery Stars) in a **male** chart is often read as ambition and leadership; in a **female** chart, the same pattern may be labeled "too strong" or "ke-fu" (harmful to husband). This asymmetry is documented in traditional texts and contemporary online fortune-telling content.

Western astrology shows parallel patterns: Venus-Saturn aspects framed as "relationship obstacles" for women, Mars strength as problematic for women but admirable for men.

## Where the debate is

- Whether divination should be studied as **culture/religion** vs. **pseudoscience**
- Whether AI should **replace** human fortune-tellers or **critique** their language
- How much young women internalize divination narratives vs. treat them as entertainment

## The gap my project fills

No existing interactive tool lets users **experience the same chart through different gender lenses** and then **actively reframe** the narrative with AI assistance. Most apps either deliver traditional readings unchanged or dismiss divination entirely. Reframe Destiny sits in the middle: **critical, not dismissive**.

## My angle

Combine **digital humanities + feminist narrative analysis + AI-assisted reframing** in one interactive artifact aimed at young women, using both BaZi and astrology to show bias is **cross-cultural**, not only "a Chinese problem."
