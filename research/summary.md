# Research Summary

## What is already known

Traditional divination systems (BaZi in Chinese culture, Western astrology globally) function as **narrative frameworks** — they tell stories about who a person is and what life should look like. Feminist and discourse theorists (Butler, Foucault, Haraway) argue such narratives can **reproduce gender norms** rather than describe neutral truths.

In BaZi, the same chart elements receive **different interpretations by gender**: strong Peer/Robbery Stars in a male chart may read as ambition; in a female chart, as "too strong" or "ke-fu." Classical texts (《三命通会》《滴天髓》) and contemporary AI/SEO fortune-telling recycle these labels. Western astrology shows parallel patterns: Venus/Mars rules assign heteronormative relationship roles; "divine feminine/masculine" TikTok discourse repackages trad gender roles as spirituality.

Young people (15–35) in China engage heavily with divination for **career and money anxiety**, not only romance — but gendered marriage narratives ("正缘," 克夫) remain prominent in BaZi and social-media divination. A 2025 Houlang Institute survey (待核实 primary report) finds 60% have tried AI fortune-telling; Bazi (60%), astrology (55.8%), and tarot (44.8%) lead practice types.

## Where the debate is

- Whether divination should be studied as **culture/narrative** vs. dismissed as pseudoscience
- Whether AI should **replace** fortune-tellers or **amplify** stereotypes in training data
- Whether young users **internalize** readings vs. treat them as entertainment — and whether a short critical interaction can shift awareness

Existing **gender-bias tools** (Gender Decoder, Lean In workshops) target workplace language, not divination. **Critical divination** projects (Divine Machine, Stars Sieves and Stories) expose ritual mechanics but rarely combine gender critique + AI reframe workflow. **AI命理 apps** (万象合参, AuraMate) optimize for insight and companionship, not bias critique.

## The gap my project fills

No public interactive product combines **Chinese BaZi + Western astrology + gender-bias tagging + AI narrative reframe** in one short user journey aimed at young people. Reframe Destiny sits in the middle: **critical, not dismissive** — questioning narrative authority without proving whether divination is "true."

## My angle

Combine **digital humanities + feminist narrative analysis + AI-assisted reframing** in one MVP artifact. Ruthlessly cut to one core loop: **read traditional text → tag bias → compare reframe → write one sentence** — measurable in ~15 minutes for a pre/post user study.
